National + State Parks

California has nine (9 (IX)) national parks! Paso is just a few hours drive from many of them. We assume you've heard of some of them, but offer a brief overview here. Check out the page header for a map from the NPS. For all of these, if you're camping, you should aim to reserve in advance (we have some tricks for getting spots, just ask!). If camping isn't your thing, many of the parks also have lodges or you can usually find an Airbnb nearby. Death Valley, Joshua Tree, and Pinnacles all will probably be quite hot — please check park conditions before you go, and always make sure to have plenty of water!

  • Pinnacles: Some nice peaks and caves, great option if you're heading to/from SF, but less exciting than some others. Closest to Paso, ~1.5 hour drive.
  • Sequoia/King's Canyon: Next closest to Paso, often done as a pair (~2.5-3 hour drive). Big trees and mountains! We've never been — invite us if you decide to go!
  • Yosemite: Also a great option (~3-3.5 hour drive from Paso). Waterfalls and mountains (Half Dome, etc etc).
  • Death Valley: Literal convection oven, but otherworldly, with salt flats and sand dunes. ~4.5-5 hours from Paso.
  • Joshua Tree: Seussian plant life and very large rocks (guaranteed to knock off your socks). ~5 hours from Paso.
  • Channel Islands: Requires a ferry boat ride, but a fun option if you're heading south. ~3 hours to the relevant coast.
  • Lassen: Far north (~6.5 hours from Paso), but fun geo/hydrothermal features. This one is also on our wish list!
  • Redwoods: Very far north (~8-9 hours from Paso), but nice trees and a Jurassic Park hike!

California also has many many state parks, as well as national recreation areas. We'll call some of them out in other areas, but Muir Woods is a famous one near San Francisco — reservations needed in advance!

"Coastal Elites"

There's a big ocean nearby. Sometimes that can be nice. With as much coastline as California has, there are many great coastal places, but we have a few areas in particular that are special to us.

There is lots of great coast close to Paso! We in particular recommend Morro Bay, a nice coastal town featuring a big rock (Morro Rock). We've also heard good things about Cambria and San Simeon. While you're in the area, head to Hearst Castle and belt Newsies in the foyer (do they got us? no!).

The site of our engagement, the first place we said "I love you"s, a place that almost was our wedding venue. Tomales Bay/Point Reyes is a beautiful region of the world ~1 hour north of San Francisco (~4 hours from Paso). Famous for oyster farming and cheese making, with a dramatic and moody coastline and many opportunities for camping, hiking, and (sometimes bioluminescent) kayaking, we really enjoy the area, and highly recommend it as a destination for an extended California trip. Be warned that it may be a little chilly, even in the summer. We have a few particular recommendations:

  • Oysters: Hog Island (most famous/provider of a cooler of oysters for our engagement/featured in an episode of the Chef Show that Sean really likes and Haley fell asleep during), Tony's (the "I love you" place), The Marshall Store
  • Cheese: Marin French (Sean loves the Breakfast Brie), Point Reyes Cheese. For more cheese check out the California Cheese Trail.
  • Miscellaneous: West Marin Culture Club (we love the mushroom miso)
  • Kayaking: Blue Waters (rentals, also offer bioluminescent tours), Marshall Beach (engagement site)
  • Camping: Check out the NPS site

A "home base" coast spot for Haley and Sean (as well as many people from the SF Peninsula) is Half Moon Bay, featuring some lovely beaches. A classic activity is to grab a mushroom sandwich from Dad's Luncheonette, a train caboose restaurant featuring a former Michelin starred chef who wanted to spend more time with his family, and then go to the nearby Francis Beach or Venice Beach (no relation).

Big Sur is a great option for those looking for a coastal drive up from Paso, though the coastal highway 1 is often closed. The area does have some nice camping and good restaurants — Haley likes the Wild Coast Restaurant for omakase.

There are a few nice coastal towns between Paso and SF, notably Carmel-by-the-Sea, Monterey (birthplace of Monterey Jack), and Santa Cruz. The Monterey Bay Aquarium is worth a stop, as is the Santa Cruz Boardwalk. AÃ±o Nuevo State Park is also a fun spot to see elephant seals, and you can stop by Pescadero for some fish tacos and artichoke soup.

Mendocino County (north of SF) also has some gems along the coast. If you're interested in a luxurious getaway, the Harbor House Inn is a beautiful spot with a private cove, walkable gardens, and a Michelin-starred restaurant serving a coastal-cuisine focused tasting menu. Glass Beach in Fort Bragg is another fun spot — it's a former dumping ground for glass, appliances, and vehicles that has now been pounded down by tumbling waves, leaving the beach covered in smooth, colourful* sea glass.

Cities

California has a few people living there. Some of those people live in concentrated areas and develop arts and restaurants and culture. Paso Robles is located right in the middle of the California coastline (the "Central Coast"), making it a great jumping off point for exploration in any direction. The closest larger city to Paso Robles is San Luis Obispo (~30 min drive). San Francisco and Los Angeles are both ~3.5 hours from Paso. San Diego is ~5.5 hours. All of these have many many options for things to do and see, so we'll leave that up to you, but feel free to reach out if you'd like any suggestions!
